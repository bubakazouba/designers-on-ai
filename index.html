Refik Anadol

Media Art / AI Data Art

Why they're established

Turkish-American media artist and pioneer of AI/data art; founder of Refik Anadol Studio (2014), former Google artist-in-residence, and creator of the solo AI installation 'Unsupervised' exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA).

Verify · wipo.int · 1 December 2024 ↗

“What happens if a machine can dream and, if it can, who defines what is real and what is not?”

— Refik Anadol · wipo.int, 1 December 2024 ↗

Beeple (Mike Winkelmann)

Digital Art

Why they're established

Digital artist behind 'Everydays: The First 5000 Days,' which sold at Christie's for $69.3 million in 2021; one of the most-followed digital artists working today. His 'Diffuse Control' is a genuine generative-AI installation (The Shed, then LACMA).

Verify · en.wikipedia.org · reference ↗

“For those who have not checked it out yet @midjourney is pretty insane. Gonna be really interesting to see how these amazing new AI tools affect art moving forward when you can literally just type a few words and make something.”

— Beeple · x.com, 28 May 2022 ↗

Chinese-Canadian pioneer of human-machine collaborative art who paints alongside AI-trained robotic arms (the D.O.U.G. project); a 2019 TED speaker whose robotic-drawing work was acquired by London's Victoria & Albert Museum (2022), and who was named to the 2023 TIME100 AI list, with international exhibitions from the Vancouver Art Gallery to the ArtScience Museum, Singapore.

“My goal isn't to replace traditional methods but to deepen and expand them, allowing art to arise from a genuine meeting of human and machine perspectives.”

Mario Klingemann

AI / GAN Art

Why they're established

German artist and pioneer of neural-network (GAN) art, a Google Arts & Culture resident (2016-2018) and 2018 Lumen Prize Gold winner; his GAN installation 'Memories of Passersby I' became the first AI artwork auctioned at Sotheby's in 2019.

Verify · en.wikipedia.org · reference ↗

“I use AI as a tool and the works that I make with this tool are mine and not a collaboration, in the same way I would not call a hammer or a piano a 'collaborator'.”

Anna Ridler

GAN / Dataset Art

Why they're established

British artist working with datasets and machine learning whose work is held in the permanent collection of the V&A; she has exhibited at the Barbican ('AI: More Than Human'), Centre Pompidou, ZKM Karlsruhe and Ars Electronica, where her tulip work received an honorary mention and was nominated for the Beazley Designs of the Year.

“For me it is very much a tool and a process. I don't find it encroaching on human expression, I find it a way of expressing myself more fully, in ways that I was never able to before.”

Scott Eaton

AI + Figure Drawing

Why they're established

British-based artist, anatomist and educator who studied figurative drawing/sculpture in Florence and computer graphics at the MIT Media Lab; his solo exhibition 'Artist+AI: Figures & Form in the Age of Intelligent Machines' was staged at Somerset House, London (2019), his ML works have shown at the Vancouver Art Gallery and NVIDIA's AI Art Gallery, and he teaches anatomy to studios including Disney, Pixar and Framestore.

Verify · aiartists.org · reference ↗

“In the near-term, AI will give artists amazing new creative capabilities.”

— Scott Eaton · thelondonmagazine.org, 26 June 2019 ↗

Pindar Van Arman

Robotic AI Painting

Why they're established

American artist and roboticist who has built AI painting robots ('cloudpainter') for ~15 years; his 'AI Imagined Portrait Painted by a Robot #2' won First Place at Robot Art 2018 and was acquired into the permanent collection of the Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA, 2022), and his practice has been profiled by TIME and NVIDIA's AI Art Gallery.

Verify · vanarman.com · reference ↗

“My machines have grown beyond being simple assistants and are now effectively augmenting my own creativity, as well as having creativity of their own.”

— Pindar Van Arman · dallasinnovates.com, 17 March 2023 ↗

How this was built

Everyone here clears three bars: (1) an established, verifiable career as an artist/designer/director — not an AI-industry executive; (2) they actively create with AI, shown through their real AI output rather than a portrait; and (3) a dated, sourced statement in support of that work. Where someone’s stance was skeptical, or no verifiable AI-work image existed, they were left off rather than padded with a headshot. Every image links to its source; every credential and quote carries its publication date.
